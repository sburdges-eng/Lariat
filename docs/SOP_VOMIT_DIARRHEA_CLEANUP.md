# Body-fluid cleanup — SOP

**Owner:** PIC on duty.
**Cite:** FDA 2022 Food Code §2-501.11; CO 6-CCR-1010-2 §2-501.11.
**Kit location:** Attested on the monthly cleaning schedule (see
`/admin/cleaning-schedule`, task "Check body-fluid cleanup kit").
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-24.

> A vomit or diarrhea event on the floor, in the dining room, in a
> restroom, or on any food-contact or utensil-contact surface is an
> immediate critical event. This SOP is the response. Every cook and
> server must know where the kit is and how to use it.

---

## 1. Stop and isolate — 60 seconds

1. **Stop service to the affected area.** Expo calls it; FOH blocks
   guests from walking through.
2. **Clear a 25-foot radius** (vomit) or **6-foot radius** (diarrhea on
   a hard surface). Rope off with chairs or caution cones from the kit.
3. **Discard all exposed food within the radius.** Plates on occupied
   tables inside the radius: discard. Open food at a station inside
   the radius: discard. Food stored in closed containers: keep.
4. **Do NOT** try to "wipe it up" first. Wiping aerosolizes norovirus
   and spreads contamination. Use the kit.

## 2. Suit up — 60 seconds

Every kit contains:
- Gown or disposable apron
- N95 or surgical mask
- Gloves (2 pairs — one over the other)
- Shoe covers
- Safety glasses

Put them on in this order: gown → mask → inner gloves → glasses →
shoe covers → outer gloves.

## 3. Contain — 2 minutes

1. **Cover the spill** with absorbent powder from the kit, or with
   paper towels if powder is out. Let it sit 60 seconds.
2. **Scoop** the absorbed material into the red biohazard bag with the
   stiff scraper. Do not mop — mopping spreads the contamination to
   the mop and the bucket.
3. **Drop the scraper in the bag.** One-use only.

## 4. Clean and sanitize — 5 minutes

1. **Wash** the contaminated surface with hot soapy water using a
   disposable towel.
2. **Sanitize** with a **bleach solution at 5000 ppm** (about 1⅔ cups
   household bleach per gallon of water — this is 10× normal sanitizer
   strength). Let it sit **at least 10 minutes** before wiping.
3. For carpet or upholstery: sanitize with the same bleach solution
   and call for professional extraction.
4. **Throw away** every towel used. Don't launder.

## 5. Dispose

1. **Double-bag.** Outer bag goes in the dumpster, not a kitchen
   trash can.
2. **Peel off PPE** in reverse order (outer gloves → shoe covers →
   glasses → inner gloves → mask → gown). Each item into the
   biohazard bag as you remove it.
3. **Wash hands** twice with soap and water — 20 seconds each — at a
   hand-wash sink, not a three-compartment sink.

## 6. After-action — PIC responsibility

1. **Check every cook and server who was on shift.** Any symptoms
   (vomiting, diarrhea, sore throat with fever, jaundice, infected
   lesion) → open a `sick_worker_report` at `/food-safety/sick-worker`
   and exclude per §2-201.11.
2. **Log the event.** Note on `/food-safety/cleaning` with area,
   cook, time, and reason "body-fluid cleanup". An inspector will
   ask for this record.
3. **Restock the kit** before end of shift. Add a new supply-order
   line to the purchasing list if anything is depleted.
4. **If the spiller was a guest with norovirus-like symptoms**,
   expect more cases in 12–48 hours. Brief the team at next pre-shift.

---

## Kit contents checklist

Keep one kit at the server station and one in the BOH cleaning
closet. Minimum stock per kit:

- [ ] 1 × disposable gown
- [ ] 2 × N95 or surgical masks
- [ ] 4 × pairs of nitrile gloves (size M + L)
- [ ] 2 × shoe covers
- [ ] 1 × safety glasses
- [ ] 2 × red biohazard bags with ties
- [ ] 1 × absorbent powder packet (≥ 8 oz)
- [ ] 1 × stiff plastic scraper (one-use)
- [ ] 6 × disposable towels
- [ ] 1 × bottle concentrated bleach (≥ 16 oz, unopened)
- [ ] 1 × measuring cup marked for 1⅔ cups
- [ ] 1 × laminated copy of this SOP

Attest monthly on the cleaning schedule — task
"Check body-fluid cleanup kit" at `/admin/cleaning-schedule`.
